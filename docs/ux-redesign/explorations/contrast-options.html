Notes on the numbers

  • Contrast ratios are based on the rendered foreground/background pair only. The numeric ratio is part of the story but not all of it -- the typography (condensed Antonio at 11px vs Geist 13px medium) materially changes how much of those pixels are colored, which is what actually drives readability for astigmatism and aging vision.
  • Counter-intuitively, Option A's 4.62:1 number is lower than the current 5.65:1, yet the perceived readability is higher. That's because cream-on-deep-red avoids the saturation shimmer that black-on-saturated-red produces, and it survives the deuteranopia simulation much better. Pure-number WCAG misses the perceptual win.
  • Option B (hollow) gives the cleanest number but loses the punch of a filled CTA. It might be right for secondary actions (Reject, Cancel) but probably too quiet for the primary "New match" / "Save & next" affordance.
  • Option C (typography only) is the smallest physical change but doesn't help colorblind users -- it just makes the same colors readable in their original size. Run the deuteranopia toggle above and the difference is clear.
  • Option D combines the perceptual win of A with the typographic win of C, and it's the only option that holds up across all four simulation toggles. The LED red (#FF2D2D) still owns brand & accent surfaces (dots, focus rings, hairlines, brand mark) -- only the filled-button background shifts to the deeper red (#DC2626).
